MoSCoW Method

Definition

A method for categorizing requirements into four buckets:

  • Must have: Non-negotiable for launch.
  • Should have: High-priority, but the product won't fail without them.
  • Could have: Nice-to-haves if resources allow.
  • Won't have: Explicitly not in the current scope.

Prioritization Guidelines

  1. Define your core business values.
  1. Identify potential risks.
  1. Edit the priority categories to fit your project's context.

Kano Model

Definition

A theory for understanding customer preferences and satisfaction, categorizing features into five types:

  • Basic: Expected features. Their absence causes dissatisfaction.
  • Performance: The more, the better. Satisfaction is proportional to functionality.
  • Delighters: Unexpected features that create high satisfaction.
  • Indifferent: Features that users don't care about.
  • Reverse: Features that cause dissatisfaction when present.

Note: Delighters often become Basic features over time as user expectations evolve.

Prioritization Guidelines

  1. Classify your features into Delighters, Performance, Basic, and Indifferent.
  1. Priority Order: Build Basic features first, then Performance features, and finally Delighters.
  1. Deprioritize or eliminate Indifferent features.

RICE Scoring

Definition

A quantitative method for scoring features based on four factors:

  • Reach: How many people will this impact per time period? (e.g., customers/quarter)
  • Impact: How much will this impact each individual user? (Scale: 3=Massive, 2=High, 1=Medium, 0.5=Low, 0.25=Minimal)
  • Confidence: How confident are you in your estimates? (Percentage: 100%=High, 80%=Medium, 50%=Low)
  • Effort: How much work will it take? (Person-months or story points)

RICE Score Formula: (Reach × Impact × Confidence) / Effort

Prioritization Guidelines

  1. Estimate Reach, Impact, Confidence, and Effort for each feature.
  1. Calculate the RICE score.
  1. Sort features by score from highest to lowest.

Feasibility, Desirability, Viability

Definition

A balanced framework that evaluates features from three key perspectives:

  • Feasibility: How technically possible is it? (Talk to engineers, designers)
  • Desirability: Do users want it? (Talk to researchers, UX, customers)
  • Viability: Does it align with business strategy? (Talk to executives, PMs)

Prioritization Guidelines

  1. Score each feature on a scale (e.g., 1-10) for Feasibility, Desirability, and Viability.
  1. Sum the scores.
  1. Prioritize features with the highest total scores.

Value/Effort Scale

Definition

A simple 2x2 matrix to map features based on the effort to build them versus the business value they deliver.

Prioritization Guidelines

  1. Rate each feature's Effort and Business Value.
  1. Plot them on the 2x2 grid.
  1. Build First: High Value, Low Effort (Quick Wins).
  1. Consider Next: High Value, High Effort (Major Projects).
  1. Deprioritize: Low Value, Low Effort (Fill-Ins).
  1. Avoid: Low Value, High Effort (Time Sinks).
