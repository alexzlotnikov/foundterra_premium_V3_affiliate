Startup Incorporation Guide

🏛 Delaware Startup Incorporation: Step-by-Step Guide

📌 Key Takeaways

  • Delaware C-Corporations are preferred for startups seeking venture capital.
  • Early planning ensures equity, IP protection, and fundraising readiness.
  • International founders can incorporate remotely with a registered agent and virtual mailbox.
  • Maintaining corporate hygiene is critical: cap table management, annual reports, and compliance reduce legal risk and support investors.

1️⃣ Planning 🔽

Planning ensures that founders understand ownership, IP rights, equity, and long-term fundraising implications. Skipping planning can cause disputes, slow fundraising, or legal issues later.

Checklist with Explanations:

  • Review employment contracts – Ensure IP created during employment does not belong to your employer. Protects your startup from legal disputes.
  • Consult a startup attorney – Incorporation is a legal process, a lawyer ensures you choose the right entity and compliance strategy. But you don’t explicitly need a lawyer for incorporation itself.
  • Consult a tax advisor - Understand your tax obligations and don’t forget to pay taxes on time.
  • Create a business/legal/funding checklist – Aligns all founders on goals, IP, equity splits, and funding plans.
  • Decide equity splits for founders – Clear ownership prevents disputes and supports investor confidence.
  • Set vesting schedules – Protects the startup if a founder leaves early; common: 4 years with a 1-year cliff.
  • Decide founder payments – Determine if shares are purchased via cash, IP, or sweat equity to formalize contributions.
  • Sign releases for “lost founders” – Ensures no one can claim equity for minimal early contributions.

When to Incorporate:

  • When creating intellectual property needing protection
  • Before onboarding co-founders, employees, or contractors
  • Before raising investor capital
  • To formalize equity ownership early

Entity Choice Considerations:

  • Delaware C-Corp ✅: Investor-friendly, flexible shares, preferred by VCs
  • Delaware LLC ⚠️: Pass-through taxation, flexible, less investor-friendly
  • Other states 🌎: Lower cost but may require foreign registration if operating in multiple states

Benefits of Delaware:

  • Business-friendly laws, flexible corporate structure
  • Chancery Court resolves disputes efficiently
  • Privacy for directors/officers
  • Investor-preferred for fundraising
  • Efficient stock issuance and scaling for future rounds

2️⃣ First Steps 🔽

First steps establish your startup legally and protect its name, legal address, and founder rights. Mistakes here can delay incorporation or create compliance issues.

Checklist with Explanations:

  • Review incorporation questionnaire – Clarifies company details for accurate filings.
  • Select registered agent – Legal requirement; they receive official notices.
  • Reserve company name (optional) – Protects your brand for 120 days while incorporating.
  • Choose sole incorporator – Person authorized to file certificate of incorporation; typically a formality.
  • Dissolve existing entity – Avoids conflicts if converting from another business structure.

International Founder Considerations:

  • U.S. mailing address (virtual mailbox) – Required for some legal notifications and receiving mail (different from Registered agent)
  • EIN – Necessary for banking and hiring.
  • Bank account – Remote fintech solutions simplify access (Wise, Mercury, Payoneer).
  • Understand U.S. tax obligations – Prevents compliance issues.
  • Registered agent fees – Ongoing legal requirement.

3️⃣ Certificate of Incorporation 🔽

The certificate establishes your company legally, defines authorized shares, and sets equity structure. It forms the foundation for fundraising and governance.

Checklist with Explanations:

  • Set authorized shares & par value – Determines total ownership capacity and valuation flexibility.
  • Allocate founder shares – Sets ownership and voting power.
  • Optional: Multiple share classes – Enables preferred stock for investors or restricted voting stock.
  • Complete certificate of incorporation – Legally registers your company.
  • File forms with state – Submission completes official formation.

Fundraising Readiness:

  • Standardize agreements & cap table – Ensures investors see clean ownership.
  • Assign IP to corporation – Protects assets before funding.
  • Obtain 409A valuation (if issuing options) – Determines fair market value for stock options.

4️⃣ Post-Incorporation 🔽

Post-incorporation actions establish governance, formalize equity, and protect the company from legal or operational risk.

Checklist with Explanations:

  • Appoint board of directors – Provides governance and decision-making authority.
  • Hold first board meeting / written consent – Approves initial corporate actions.
  • Sign founders’ stock purchase agreements – Formalizes contributions and ownership.
  • Adopt bylaws – Defines how your company operates and how the board functions.
  • Draft shareholder agreement – Protects rights and responsibilities of shareholders.
  • Issue stock certificates (optional) – Creates formal ownership record.
  • Obtain certificates of good standing – Required for banking or investor due diligence.
  • Register as foreign corporation – Required if operating outside Delaware.
  • Sign CIIAAs – Protects IP created by founders and employees.
  • Create stock incentive plan (optional) – Framework for employee stock options.
  • Maintain minute book – Records board/shareholder actions for legal protection.
  • Comply with state & local regulations – Avoid fines and maintain business licenses.
  • Optional: Terms of Use / Privacy Policy – Protects the business and meets regulatory requirements.

Post-Incorporation Essentials:

  • Employment agreements
  • Stock option pool
  • Insurance coverage (General Liability & D&O)
  • Recordkeeping (digital minute book, cap table)

5️⃣ Financial Requirements 🔽

Ensures legal compliance, proper tax reporting, and correct handling of funds. Prevents penalties and investor concerns.

Checklist with Explanations:

  • Open corporate bank account – Separates personal and company funds.
  • Avoid S-Corp election – Restricts venture funding options.
  • File Section 83(b) election for vesting shares – Protects founders from additional taxes.
  • Check securities law requirements – Avoid legal violations for stock issuance.
  • File annual report & franchise tax – Maintains good standing with Delaware.
  • Optional filings: Form D (SEC) / State “blue sky” filings

❓ FAQs 🔽

  • When to incorporate: When building IP, onboarding co-founders, or raising capital.
  • Is Delaware mandatory? No, but preferred for fundraising.
  • Can international founders incorporate remotely? Yes, with registered agent and virtual mailbox.
  • Consequences of late incorporation: Ownership disputes, IP issues, investor challenges.
  • Best entity type for venture-backed startups: Delaware C-Corporation.
